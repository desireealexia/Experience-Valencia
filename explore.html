Top Attractions in Valencia

Explore the must-see sights of Valencia. Discover the iconic City of Arts and Sciences, stroll through the historic Old Town, and relax in the lush Turia Gardens.

City of Arts and Sciences

This cutting-edge complex is a masterpiece of contemporary design, featuring Europe's largest aquarium.



Address: Quatre Carreres, 46013 Valencia, Spain

The Silk Exchange

The Silk Exchange is one of Europe's most stunning gothic structures with its magnificent architecture.



Address: C/ de la Llotja, 2, Ciutat Vella, 46001 València, Valencia, Spain

Valencia Cathedral

With the history of being a Roman temple turned into a mosque, this gothic style building is worth the visit.



Address: Pl. de l'Almoina, Ciutat Vella, 46003 València, Valencia, Spain

Bioparc Valencia

An innovative zoo where animals live in natural habitats. Explore the African savannahs, wetlands, and rainforests.



Address: Av. Pío Baroja, 3, Campanar, 46015 València, Valencia, Spain

Valencia's Culinary Delights

Indulge in the rich flavours of Valencia. From the famous paella to a variety of fresh seafood and traditional tapas, the city's culinary scene is a feast for the senses.

Paella

Why not try traditional Valencian paella with rabbit and chicken for an authentic experience.



Where to try: Barraca de Toni Montoliu or Bon Aire

Horchata

A drink made from tiger nuts, sugar, and water, and pairs perfectly with sweet pastries like fartons.



Where to try: Horchateria Santa Catalina or Horchateria Daniel

Churros and Chocolate

Locals tend to dunk churros - sugared, deep-fried batter sticks - into thick hot dark drinking chocolate.



Where to try: Horchateria Fabian or Choclateria Valor

Auga de Valencia

It is a local cocktail made from the juice of Valencian oranges, cava, gin and vodka. It is a must try while in Valencia.



Where to try: Cafe de las Horas or Cafe Lisboa
