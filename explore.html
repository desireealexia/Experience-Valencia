Top Attractions in Valencia

Explore the must-see sights of Valencia. Discover the iconic City of Arts and Sciences, stroll through the historic Old Town, and relax in the lush Turia Gardens.

City of Arts and Sciences

This futuristic complex is a marvel of modern architecture. It includes the impressive Oceanogràfic aquarium, the interactive Science Museum, and the stunning Hemisférico planetarium.

Address: Quatre Carreres, 46013 Valencia, Spain

The Silk Exchange

The Silk Exhnage is one of Europe's most stunning gothic structures with its magnificent architecture. It is National Historic and Artistic Monument and recognised as a World Heritage Site.

Address: C/ de la Llotja, 2, Ciutat Vella, 46001 València, Valencia, Spain

Valencia Cathedral

Located in the old town is the Cathedral of Valencia. With the history of being a Roman temple turned into a mosque and its exhibition of works by Goya and Maella, this gothic style building is worth the visit.

Address: Pl. de l'Almoina, s/n, Ciutat Vella, 46003 València, Valencia, Spain

Bioparc Valencia

Bioparc Valencia is an innovative zoo where animals live in natural habitats. Explore the African savannahs, wetlands, and rainforests, observing a diverse range of species including elephants, lions and gorillas.

Address: Av. Pío Baroja, 3, Campanar, 46015 València, Valencia, Spain

Valencia's Culinary Delights

Indulge in the rich flavours of Valencia. From the famous paella to a variety of fresh seafood and traditional tapas, the city's culinary scene is a feast for the senses.

Paella

A delicious rice dish cooked with saffron, vegetables, and various proteins. Try traditional Valencian paella with rabbit and chicken for an authentic experience.

Where to try: Barraca de Toni Montoliu or Bon Aire

Horchata

A refreshing drink made from tiger nuts, sugar, and water. It's a local specialty that pairs perfectly with sweet pastries like fartons.

Where to try: Horchateria Santa Catalina or Horchateria Daniel

Churros and Chocolate

A beloved Spanish snack mainly eaten in winter, locals tend to dunk churros - sugared, deep-fried batter sticks - into thick hot dark drinking chocolate.

Where to try: Horchateria Fabian or Choclateria Valor

Auga de Valencia

It may not sound like anything special but it is a local cocktail made from the juice of Valencian oranges, cava, gin and vodka. It is a must try while in Valencia.

Where to try: Cafe de las Horas or Cafe Lisboa
